Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 14, 2026

Auditly is published by Tablesand LLC, a Colorado limited liability company (“Tablesand,” “we,” “us,” or “our”). This Privacy Policy explains how the Auditly app handles your information. We treat your supplement and health-context information as sensitive, and we built Auditly so that it stays on your device.

Auditly is currently offered only in the United States. If you do not agree with this policy, please do not use the app.

Our approach: on-device by design

Auditly has no account system and no user database. The supplement stack you build and the audits you run stay on your device. We do not have servers that hold your stack, and we cannot see it. A small amount of information leaves your device only for the limited purposes described below, and your health-context answers are never among it.

Information that stays on your device

The following is stored only on your device, protected by your device’s standard data protection, and in any backups you choose to make (such as iCloud Backup, if you have it enabled). We never receive it:

  • The supplements, doses, forms, and costs you enter.
  • Your goals.
  • Any optional health-context answers you provide (for example, whether you take medications, are pregnant or breastfeeding, or have a health condition).
  • The audit reports Auditly generates and anything you save.

Information that leaves your device, and who receives it

  • Automatic label reading. When you scan a supplement label, the photo is read for text on your device. Only the extracted text — not the photo, your goals, or your health-context answers — is sent to our cloud function and to Google’s Gemini service so it can be structured into ingredients and doses that you then confirm. Photos are not uploaded or stored by us. Google processes this text as our service provider on a paid tier under terms that do not permit using it to train its models, and it is not used to build an advertising profile.
  • Crash and diagnostic data. We use Google Firebase Crashlytics to collect crash and diagnostic data so we can fix stability problems. This is kept separate from your supplement and health inputs.
  • Purchases. Access passes are processed by Apple through the App Store. We receive only your access entitlement — never your name, card number, or payment details.
  • App-integrity checks. Auditly uses Apple App Attest (via Firebase App Check) to confirm that requests come from a genuine copy of the app and to prevent abuse. These tokens contain no personal data.

How we use information

  • To build your stack and run audits on your device.
  • To structure scanned label text into ingredients and doses for your confirmation.
  • To diagnose crashes and improve the app’s reliability and accuracy.
  • To process your purchases through Apple.

Third-party service providers

  • Google (Firebase Cloud Functions, App Check, Gemini, and Crashlytics): structures extracted label text, verifies app integrity, and collects crash diagnostics. See Google’s privacy policy at policies.google.com/privacy.
  • Apple: processes purchases through the App Store under Apple’s terms. See apple.com/legal/privacy.

We do not share your information with any other third parties.

What we never do

  • We never sell your personal or health data.
  • We do not run ads, use ad-based monetization, or use any analytics SDK.
  • We do not use your health inputs for marketing.

Data retention

Because your stack and reports live on your device, you control how long they are kept — they remain until you delete them or remove the app. Label text sent for structuring is processed transiently to return a result and is not retained by us. Crash diagnostics are retained by our provider for a limited period to help us fix problems.

Security

Your on-device data is protected by your device’s standard data protection. Information sent for label structuring, app-integrity checks, and crash reporting is transmitted over encrypted connections. No method of storage or transmission is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

Your choices and controls

  • Access. Everything you enter — your stack, doses, goals, and reports — stays visible to you directly in the app; that is your copy of it. We hold no server-side copy to provide.
  • Delete. You can delete all of your data at any time from Settings. Deletion removes your stack and reports from your device. Because we do not store this data, deletion is complete for what we hold. It cannot reach crash diagnostics already sent to our crash-reporting provider, or label text already processed transiently by the structuring service.

Your U.S. state privacy rights

Depending on where you live (for example California, Colorado, Virginia, or Connecticut), you may have rights to know about, access, correct, and delete your personal information, to opt out of its sale or of targeted advertising, to limit the use of sensitive information, and not to be treated differently for exercising these rights. Some states also allow you to appeal a decision.

Auditly has no account and keeps no server-side copy of your stack, reports, or health-context answers — that information lives only on your device, where you control it directly. Because of this, for most of these rights there is nothing on our end to retrieve, correct, or erase: everything you enter is visible to you in the app (that is your copy of it), and you can delete all of it from Settings, at any time, yourself. If you send us a request, we will respond as the law requires — for access or deletion, by confirming that we hold no server-side copy of your information and directing you to these in-app controls; for a copy of your data in a portable format, email us and we will work with you given that the data is on your device. You may use an authorized agent, and you may appeal our response by replying to it, which we will reconsider. We do not “sell” or “share” personal information as those terms are defined under California law, and we run no targeted advertising, so there is nothing to opt out of.

Consumer health data

Supplement information can be treated as “consumer health data” under some state laws, including in Washington and Nevada. We describe how we handle it, and the additional rights of Washington and Nevada residents, in our separate Consumer Health Data Privacy Policy.

Children

Auditly is intended for adults and is not directed to children. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child under 13 has used Auditly, contact us at privacy@tablesand.com and we will address it.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the app evolves. Material changes will be reflected on this page with a new “Last updated” date.
