Consumer Health Data Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 13, 2026

This Consumer Health Data Privacy Policy explains how Tablesand LLC, the maker of Auditly, handles “consumer health data” as that term is used under certain U.S. state laws, including the Washington My Health My Data Act and Nevada’s consumer health data law. It supplements our main Privacy Policy.

What we mean by consumer health data

Some state laws treat information that relates to a person’s health — including the supplements they take or are interested in — as consumer health data. The supplement information and optional health-context answers you enter in Auditly may fall within these definitions.

How Auditly handles it

  • Your on-device data is never collected by us. The supplements, doses, costs, goals, reports, and any health-context answers (such as medication use, pregnancy, or health conditions) you enter are stored only on your device. We have no account system and no servers that hold this data, we never receive it, and we cannot access it — so we do not collect, process, sell, or share it as those terms are used under these laws.
  • Label text is processed only to give you the scan you asked for. When you use automatic label reading, only the text read from a product label — not your health-context answers, goals, or photos — is sent to our service provider (Google) to structure it into ingredients and doses that you then confirm. We process that text because it is necessary to provide the label-scanning feature you requested; it is used for no other purpose, is not retained by us, and is handled under terms that do not permit using it to train models or to advertise.
  • We do not sell it. We never sell consumer health data. We have no mechanism for a consumer to authorize a sale, because no sale ever occurs.
  • We do not share it. We do not disclose consumer health data to any third party for that party’s own use. Google acts only as our processor, on our instructions, and never receives your health-context answers.
  • No geofencing. We do not use geofencing around any health-care facility, or for any other purpose.

Categories, sources, purposes, and sharing

To the extent any information you provide is treated as consumer health data under state law, this is how we handle it:

  • Categories we collect off your device: only the text of a supplement label you choose to scan (for example, ingredient names and amounts). We do not collect your stack, doses, goals, reports, or health-context answers, because those never leave your device.
  • Sources: the product label you photograph; its text is extracted on your device before any of it is sent.
  • Purposes: solely to structure that label text into ingredients and doses for your confirmation — the feature you requested. There is no secondary use.
  • Categories we share, and with whom: we share the label text only with Google, acting as our processor, to perform that structuring. We do not share consumer health data with any third party for that party’s own purposes, we do not sell it, and we have no affiliates that receive it.

Who we share data with

We do not share consumer health data with third parties for their own use. We rely on Google (Firebase and Gemini) only as a processor to structure label text, and on Apple to process purchases. These providers act on our instructions and do not receive your health-context answers.

Your rights

If you live in a state that grants consumer-health-data rights, you may have the right to confirm whether we process your consumer health data, access it, withdraw consent and have it deleted, and appeal a decision on your request. Here is how each works given that your data stays on your device:

  • Confirm and access. We can confirm that we hold no consumer health data about you on our servers — your stack, reports, and health-context answers never leave your device, and the only thing that does (label text during a scan) is not retained by us. There is therefore nothing on our side to provide; your own data stays visible to you in the app at any time.
  • Delete and withdraw consent. You delete your data yourself in Settings, which removes it from your device. Because we keep no copy, there is nothing further for us to delete.
  • Appeal. If you disagree with how we respond to a request, you may appeal by replying to our response, and we will reconsider.

To make a request, or to have us confirm our handling, email privacy@tablesand.com; we will verify and respond as applicable law requires. Washington residents who believe their rights have been violated may also contact the Washington State Office of the Attorney General.
